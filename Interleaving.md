When multiple different but related skills are practiced within one session. This is in contrast to the more common method of isolating one skill at a time for an extended period "blocked practice." Blocked practice [helps with initial acquisition, but harms retention compared to interleaved practice.](https://bjorklab.psych.ucla.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/13/2016/07/Simon_RBjork_2001.pdf)  There may be a place for blocked practice when getting started on a topic, but interleaving is probably preferable in general.
